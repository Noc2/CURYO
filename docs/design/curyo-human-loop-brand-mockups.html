Brand exploration

Abstract human loop visuals for Curyo

A cleaner direction for replacing the literal robot coworker with more abstract human + AI collaboration imagery. Each option keeps the black Curyo field, desktop computer, orange coffee mug, and orbit colors, but reduces anatomy and robot details so the image feels more brand-ownable.

Recommended path: Abstract Concept A.

The first option keeps the product story readable while turning the AI into a luminous partner form instead of a mascot. It should be the strongest base for landing hero crops and later logo experiments.

Abstract Concept A

Loop Partner

Recommended

Best overall balance: the human is reduced to clean contour lines, the AI reads as an intelligent orbit form, and the desktop plus mug keep the story grounded.

  • Most abstract without losing the collaboration read.
  • AI feels closer to the current Curyo orb language.
  • Cleanest foundation for a future animated hero.
Abstract Concept B

Friendliest

Friendlier and more character-like. The AI is still abstracted by loops and glow, but this version keeps a clearer assistant personality.

  • Most immediately approachable.
  • Good if the brand should feel warmer and less mysterious.

Abstract Concept C

Orbit Review

Most abstract

Strongest abstraction. The human has almost no facial detail, and the AI is mostly a glowing network cloud. It may need a little compositional tightening, but the style is distinctive.

  • Best answer to the anatomy and hair concerns.
  • Most premium and least cartoon-like.
  • AI orbit form is large enough to become a brand motif.

Prompt Direction

Generate the full scene as one image, but avoid literal character design. Ask for a partial human contour, hands at the work surface, a desktop monitor with stand, an orange mug, and an AI presence made from an orbital capsule, nodes, and soft glow.

Useful Constraints

The key negative prompts are no laptop, no literal robot body, no detailed face, no oversized hair, no text, and black background.

Sources Used

  • Google PAIR: Feedback + Control for the emphasis on feedback, user control, and trust.
  • Microsoft Research: Human-AI Interaction Guidelines for capability clarity, correction, and ongoing user feedback.
  • Stanford HAI: Humans in the Loop for framing human-in-the-loop systems as collaborative tools, not full automation.

Next Iteration

Start from Concept A if the homepage needs a clear product story. Start from Concept C if the brand should lean more premium and symbolic. In either case, edit one thing at a time: crop, AI shape, then hand position.
