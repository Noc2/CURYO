Human loop visuals for Curyo

Three original directions for replacing the abstract orb with a friendlier AI + human collaboration language. Each keeps Curyo's dark product shell, warm coral action color, and the loop motif, but makes the human judgment layer visible on first glance.

Recommended path: Desk Loop, with the black production treatment.

This is the friendliest read: an AI helper sits beside a human reviewer, while the Curyo-colored loop keeps the existing brand energy. The black field and white line work make it fit the current landing page more directly.

Implementation notes

  • Keep the current orb as a fallback asset until the new mark is tested at favicon, header, hero, and social-card sizes.
  • Prototype Concept B first by replacing CuryoAnimation with a vector hero component and swapping CuryoLogo to an SVG mark.
  • Use animation sparingly: a slow loop stroke draw or subtle handoff pulse is enough.
  • Run visual checks on mobile and laptop widths before shipping, especially the hero height and header logo clarity.

Research cues used

The mockups lean on human-in-the-loop UX patterns that emphasize selective human participation, clear decision points, feedback capture, and trust calibration. Visually, that means the human review action must be as visible as the AI.

  • Human in the loop as an HCI design problem, not just automation.
  • AI asks for help at defined decision points, then resumes with human feedback.
  • Interfaces should make handoffs, control, and result confidence visible.
